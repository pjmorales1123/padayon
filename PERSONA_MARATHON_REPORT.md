### Student: Explain photosynthesis
_Intent:_ teach_topic · _Subject:_ Science · _Topic:_ Photosynthesis

Great question! Let's break it down simply.

**Photosynthesis** is how plants make their own food. Think of a plant like a little chef in your garden. It uses three ingredients: sunlight, water (from the soil), and carbon dioxide (from the air). Inside the leaves, there's a special green part called chlorophyll that catches sunlight. The plant then cooks these ingredients into sugar (its food) and releases oxygen as a byproduct — the same oxygen we breathe.

A common misconception is that plants "eat" soil. Actually, they use soil mainly for support and minerals, but their real food comes from **Photosynthesis** using sunlight.

**Example:** A kamote (sweet potato) plant in your backyard — its leaves are like solar panels. Without sunlight, it can't make food and will eventually wilt.

Now, here's a guiding question to check your understanding:  
**What do you think would happen to a plant if it only got water and carbon dioxide but no sunlight?**
